Rivers We Run

Main Fork of the Salmon

Deep in the Frank Church Wilderness of Idaho, the Main Fork of the Salmon flows through steep, forested canyons and quiet sandy beaches. Because it is a free-flowing river, higher early-season water brings faster, colder runs, while later in the summer the pace slows and the water warms. On this stretch you will see rapids ranging from gentle Class I waves to exciting Class IV drops, along with wildlife and beautiful camps.

Desolation & Gray Canyon

This 84-mile stretch of the Green River winds between Sand Wash and Swaseys. High gray cliffs, layered desert rock, and side canyons tell the story of both geology and early river travelers. Desolation camp; Gray is usually run in six days and features mostly Class II and III rapids, making it a great balance of fun whitewater, scenery, and time to relax on the river.
