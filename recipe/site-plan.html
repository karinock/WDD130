Overview

Purpose

The purpose of my website is to share my favorite homemade recipes and help others enjoy easy, delicious meals. Each page will feature step-by-step instructions, personal notes, and photos of dishes I’ve made. My goal is to show that cooking can be simple, fun, and rewarding for anyone, even busy students.

Audience

The primary audience for Ouchi Gohan includes home cooks, students, and food enthusiasts who enjoy simple, comforting Japanese meals made with everyday ingredients. Visitors are looking for easy-to-follow recipes, cultural tips, and warm inspiration to bring the taste of Japan into their own kitchens.

Content

Home page

Welcome to Ouchi Gohan (おうちごはん).

In Japanese, “Ouchi Gohan” means “home-cooked meal.” At Ouchi Gohan, we believe that even the simplest dishes can bring warmth, joy, and connection to the table. Whether you are cooking for friends, family, or just yourself, our recipes are made to be comforting and easy to follow.

Here, you will find ideas and inspiration to recreate everyday Japanese meals right at home. No fancy tools required. Slow down, cook with love, and enjoy the beauty of simple home-style food.

Images for the Home page

Taste of Home

Taste of Home — Easy Japanese Recipes.

Discover delicious Japanese dishes you can make in your own kitchen. From miso soup and onigiri to teriyaki chicken and curry rice, our recipes are simple, quick, and introduce authentic flavors using ingredients you can find in most grocery stores.

Each recipe includes helpful tips and personal stories to make cooking more enjoyable. Try them out, add your own twist, and share a meal with someone you care about. Great food starts with small steps and love.

Images for "Taste of Home"

Kitchen Basics & Ingredients

Kitchen Basics & Must-Have Ingredients.

Getting started with Japanese home cooking is easier than you think. All you really need are a few key ingredients like soy sauce, miso, mirin, dashi, plus everyday tools like a rice cooker, sharp knife, and a basic pot or pan.

On this page, we will teach you simple techniques such as how to measure rice, how to create rich flavor using dashi, and how to store essentials properly. Start small, cook with confidence, and build your own Japanese-style kitchen one step at a time.

Wireframes

Create three wireframes for your site. One for each page and list them here

Home

The Home page features a full-width header with the Ouchi Gohan logo on the left and navigation links on the right. A large hero banner introduces the site theme with the title “Ouchi Gohan (おうちごはん)” and a short subtitle. Below the hero, a welcome section explains the meaning and warmth of Japanese home-style cooking. The next section includes three feature boxes—Easy Recipes, Learn the Basics, and Cook with Heart—each with an image and label, linking users to deeper content. A final introductory message further explains the spirit of Japanese home kitchens. The page uses consistent fonts, full-width sections, and generous whitespace, ending with a complete footer containing links and social icons.

[Page 2]

The Recipes page keeps the same header and navigation for consistency. A medium hero banner introduces the page with the title “Recipes | Taste of Home.” The main section displays a grid of recipe cards, each containing an image, dish name in Japanese and English, and a short description. Below the grid, a two-column section pairs a text block about the meaning behind home cooking with a vertical image. This page removes category blocks to preserve a full-width footer, maintaining strong visual balance. Typography, spacing, and layout follow the design patterns of the home page to create a cohesive experience.

[Page 3]

The Essentials page starts with the shared header, followed by a wide hero banner titled “Kitchen Essentials | Get Started.” The first section displays three ingredient boxes—soy sauce, miso, and mirin—arranged in a clean three-column layout with simple icons or placeholders. Below this, a two-column layout presents essential kitchen tools, with an image of a rice cooker on the left and a bullet list of tools on the right. A full-width text section explains beginner techniques such as measuring rice, making dashi, and storing ingredients. The page ends with a callout box encouraging users to start their cooking journey and a consistent full-width footer.
