Kitchen Basics & Ingredients

Start your Japanese cooking journey with a few simple essentials—no special equipment required.

Ingredients Basics

Soy Sauce (醤油)

Adds salty, savory depth to many dishes. Use it in marinades, stir-fries, and sauces.

Miso (味噌)

A fermented soybean paste used in soups, dressings, and glazes. It gives a rich, comforting flavor.

Mirin (みりん)

A slightly sweet cooking rice wine that balances salty flavors and adds shine to sauces.

Tools You Need

  • Rice cooker (or a pot for cooking rice)
  • Sharp knife
  • Cutting board
  • Basic pot and pan
  • Small strainer or sieve

With just these tools, you can prepare most of the recipes on Ouchi Gohan. You do not need to buy everything at once—start with what you have and add tools slowly over time.

Learn the Basics

We will guide you through simple techniques like measuring rice correctly, building flavor with dashi, and storing ingredients safely. These foundations make cooking smoother and help each dish taste better.

Anyone can begin cooking Japanese food—start with the basics, cook at your own pace, and enjoy each small improvement.
